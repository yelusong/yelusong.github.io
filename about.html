Jeff Koons Looks: Looking Back on a Life in the Art World

Jeffrey Lynn Koons born in 21 January 1955, is an American artist recognized for his work dealing with po-pular culture and his sculptures depicting everyday o-bjects, including balloon animals produced in stainle-ss steel with mirror finish surfaces.

Born in York, Pennsylvania, Jeff Koons rose to promi-nence in the mid-1980s as part of a generation of arti-sts who explored the meaning of art in a media satur-ated era. He gained recognition in the 1980s and su-bsequently set up a factory-like studio in a SoHo loft on the corner of Houston Street and Broadway in Ne-w York.

In order to support himself during his early years in college, Koons would go to the MoMA every day in order to ask if there
were any job openings available for him. Finally, the opportunity was given to him asthey needed someone on the member-
ship desk. Besides interacting with people, Koons was able to study the collection and meet lots of other young artists. He
now resides and works in both New York City and his hometown of York, Pennsylvania. His works have sold for substantial
sums, including at least two record auction prices for a work by a living artist. Koons continues to create art and has no pla-
ns to lay down his material any time soon.
